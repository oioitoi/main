cottontail trail

Image from Concord Monitor published July 20,2024

Loved hearing the story about Poppy Rose a New Hampshire adopted rescue rabbit who accompanied her owner Bree Schuette and her son Aiden on mountain hikes. Schuette first encouraged her son to take a hike after the death of his father, in order to scatter his father’s ashes. Hikes became a daily activity in 2020 and were an enjoyable way to counter their grief and social isolation during the pandemic. The rabbit also brought joy to Aiden’s life, so Schuette sought to combine the two—Poppy Rose becoming their regular hiking companion.

oioitoi rabbit push-toy

Once an avid and experienced hiker, Schuette began to suffer from a rare and debilitating autoimmune disease (relapsing polychondritis) which completely ended her hiking in 2023. Poppy Rose, however, continued hiking with friends and volunteers after Schuette posted hiking companion requests to local hiking Facebook pages. Schuette wrote a book “Poppy Rose, The Hoppy Adventures of a Tiny Mountain Bunny” to tell the bunny’s tale and to help raise funds for her medical treatment.
